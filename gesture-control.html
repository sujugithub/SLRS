Landmark geometry

Finger extension is determined by comparing the Y-coordinate of each fingertip landmark to its PIP (proximal interphalangeal) joint. When the tip is above the PIP joint in normalised image coordinates, the finger is considered extended.

⚡

Swipe detection

The last 10 X-positions of the index fingertip are buffered each frame. A swipe fires when the velocity (positional delta between oldest and newest samples) exceeds 0.04 normalised units per frame, giving snappy yet noise-resistant detection.

🔇

Debouncing

Every gesture type is individually debounced at 800 ms so that holding a gesture doesn't fire the action dozens of times per second. The cursor-move gesture is exempt from debouncing since it needs to track continuously.
